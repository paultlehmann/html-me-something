Not technically a water fountain at all, but rather just a sink tap, this entry makes the top three nonetheless due to its impeccably reliable ice-cold temperatures and strong, solid flow. I'm sure all you fountain aficionados out there saw this one coming a mile away. Brookdale Senior Center has defined itself over the years not just as a reliable place to store elderly relatives, but even more so as the premier place in town to get a drink of water. The dual fountains (the one on the left is the original; its slightly-lower twin was installed in 2017 to cope with frequent lines) have become a local Mecca of sorts, with thirsty travelers driving as much as an hour to sip from its spout. And with good reason: you simply cannot find this combination of temperature and flow anywhere else. Accessibility is a breeze, with the only challenge sometimes being leaving if you have attracted a flock of seniors. The negative impact this can have on the drinking environment is really the only criticism that can possibly be leveled at this fountain.
